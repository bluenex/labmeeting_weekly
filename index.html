AGATHE: A tool for personalized rehabilitation of cognitive functions based on simulated activities of daily living

Week 7

Introduction

Cognitive Impairments

Can be from
  • Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) - Around 25,000 people in France
  • Stroke - Around 130,000 people in France
  • Degenerative

Virtual Reality

  • The use of VR for cognitive intervention is still at the stage of exploratory research


Objectives

  • To provide therapists with an innovative means of dealing with cognitive rehabilitation.
  • To offer patients customized rehabilitation sessions, on the basis of simulated activities of daily living
  • To fit into accessibility prospects of the tool (for everyone, everywhere and anywhere) and reduction of the cost of rehabilitation
  • To open a new application field to the technology of the industrial partners.

Consortium

  • Conceived and coordinated by Arts et MeÃÅtiers ParisTech
  • The expertise of this project leader is in virtual reality therapeutic applications
  • Six partners with complementary multidisciplinary skills worked together to design and produce the AGATHE tool
  • The Research Team included partners from virtual reality specialists, rehabilitation clinicians, occupational therapists and neuropsychologists

Participants

  • AGATHE is built focusing on acquired brain injury, especially TBI and stroke
  • Patients and therapists were recruited in the rehabilitation service of the University Hospital of Bordeaux and that of Kerpape rehabilitation center.

Methods

  • AGATHE tool is a software package dedicated to two types of users (patients and therapists)
  • Based on two real-time softwares, 3DVIA Studio from Dassault Systemes (creation of real-time virtual interactive environments) and RTMaps from Intempora (monitoring and real-time recording of patient activity through sensors)
